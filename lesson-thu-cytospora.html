Cytospora Canker
The opportunist that punishes stressed trees — biology, ID, and what you can actually do about it

📋 Quick Reference — Cytospora Canker

ConifersNorway spruce, blue spruce, white spruce, ponderosa pine
Stone fruitPeach, plum, cherry, apricot (Okanagan orchards)
Poplars/WillowsCottonwood, trembling aspen, hybrid poplar
Other broadleafSiberian elm, Manitoba maple (less commonly)
🌲 Conifer Symptoms
  • Resinous, orange-amber pitch oozing from bark
  • Needles discolour from tips inward
  • Branch death — lower canopy first
  • Sunken, discoloured bark at infection site
  • Pycnidia (orange tendrils) in wet weather
🌸 Stone Fruit / Broadleaf Symptoms
  • Gum or sap oozing from bark (gummosis)
  • Sunken, dark-bordered cankers on branches
  • Wood beneath canker: tan-brown discoloration
  • Branch death beyond canker
  • Bark "target" pattern in cross-section
