Mountain Pine Beetle

Dendroctonus ponderosae Hopkins — Family Curculionidae, Scolytinae

The bark beetle behind the largest recorded insect outbreak in BC's history — 18+ million hectares of pine killed. Know the biology, recognize the stages, assess the hazard.

📋 Attack Stage Reference

🌲
Green Attack
Season 1: July–Oct
  • Crown looks healthy
  • White/pink pitch tubes on lower bole
  • Frass boring dust at base
  • Fresh galleries under bark
  • Tree is DYING
🍂
Red Attack
Season 2: following spring/summer
  • Reddish-brown needles
  • Tree is DEAD
  • Beetles have emerged
  • Blue-stain in sapwood
  • No pitch tubes (old)
🪨
Grey Attack
Season 3–5+
  • Needles dropped
  • Grey-silver snag
  • Root decay begins
  • Structural risk HIGH
  • Widow-maker hazard
Primary Hosts
Lodgepole pine, ponderosa pine, western white pine, whitebark pine
Life Cycle
Univoltine (1 year); adults fly Jul–Aug; overwinter as larvae
Flight Threshold
~16°C, calm winds; typically evening flights
Kill Mechanism
Gallery girdling (phloem) + blue-stain fungus (xylem block)
Natural Control
Sustained cold < −25°C for 3+ days kills overwintering larvae
BC Outbreak Scale
~18.1 M ha killed 1999–2015+; largest recorded insect outbreak in N. America
