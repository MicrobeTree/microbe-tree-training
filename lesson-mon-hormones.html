Plant Hormones

How auxin, cytokinin, ethylene, ABA, and gibberellin control tree growth — and why every pruning cut is a hormonal event.

📋 Hormone Quick Reference

Hormone Produced In Primary Function Arborist Relevance
Auxin (IAA) Apical meristems, young leaves Apical dominance, root initiation, cell elongation Pruning response, epicormic growth, callus formation
Cytokinin Root tips (primary source) Cell division, delays senescence, lateral bud break Root–shoot communication, canopy health signal
Ethylene All tissues, ↑ under stress Abscission, fruit ripening, stress signaling Stress indicator, premature leaf drop, senescence
Abscisic Acid (ABA) Roots, leaves (water-stressed) Stomatal closure, dormancy induction Drought response, winter hardening, transplant stress
Gibberellin (GA) Young leaves, root tips, seeds Stem elongation, seed germination, flowering Shoot extension rate, internode spacing
