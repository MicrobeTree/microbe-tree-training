Week 1 · CODIT — Compartmentalization of Decay in Trees

📋 Quick Reference — Shigo's CODIT Model

Wall 1
Vertical Barrier
Plugs vessels above & below wound (tyloses, gums). Limits vertical spread of decay. Weakest wall.
Wall 2
Growth Ring Boundary
Annual rings act as barriers to inward spread. Moderate strength.
Wall 3
Ray Boundary
Ray tissue limits lateral spread. Moderate strength.
Wall 4
Barrier Zone ⭐
Formed AFTER wounding. Separates old wood from new. Strongest wall — the real defense.
Key insight: Trees don't "heal" wounds — they wall them off. Wall 4 (the barrier zone) is laid down in new wood after the wound occurs. Decay in old wood stays in old wood — if Wall 4 holds. This is why flush cuts are catastrophic: they damage the ridge of cambium where Wall 4 forms, eliminating the tree's primary defense. — Shigo, 1984; ISA Certification Study Guide Ch. 4
